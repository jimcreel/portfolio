About Jim:

I'm a former English professor and horror film scholar. I taught technical writing and rhetoric for eleven years at the University of Wyoming, and always loved how code can provide discrete, quantifiable solutions to complex problems. During that pandemic, I taught myself python and built a web scraper and notification bot to help us build our collection of pins and Christmas ornaments. It was during this time that I discovered I have a weird fondness for data structures, APIs and ETL, and learned JavaScript. I enrolled in General Assembly to expand my experience with different frameworks en route to rebranding myself as a developer.
